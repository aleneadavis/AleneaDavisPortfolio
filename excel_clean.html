Data Cleaning and Preparation for Bike Buyers Dataset

Overview:

This project involved preparing a customer bike purchasing dataset for exploratory data analysis (EDA). The dataset, containing 1,026 records, required cleaning to standardize categorical data, remove special characters, and classify customers into age brackets for more meaningful analysis. After addressing missing data and ensuring consistency, the dataset was ready for insights into how factors like income, age, and gender influenced bike purchases.

Data Summary:

  • The dataset contains 1,026 records detailing customer demographics and purchase behavior.
  • Key fields include Gender, Marital Status, Age, Occupation, Income, Commute Distance, and Bike Purchase Status.
  • The data required significant cleaning, including removing special characters from numeric fields and standardizing categorical variables.
  • Over 30% of the data had missing values, which were handled by either filling in with context-appropriate values or removing incomplete rows.

1. Standardizing Categorical Data

To ensure consistency across the dataset, I standardized entries in the following columns:

  • Gender: Converted abbreviations ('M' and 'F') to full terms ("Male" and "Female").
  • Marital Status: Transformed 'M' and 'S' into "Married" and "Single" for clearer distinction.

3. Age Group Classification

To facilitate demographic analysis, I created a new column called Age Brackets that categorized individuals into three distinct age groups: Old, Middle Age, and Adolescent. The classification was based on the following formula:

=IF(L2>54,"Old",IF(L2>=31,"Middle Age",IF(L2<31,"Adolescent","Invalid")))
  • Old: Age > 54
  • Middle Age: 31 ≤ Age ≤ 54
  • Adolescent: Age < 31

4. Text Formatting

Consistency was ensured in fields like "Occupation" and "Region" by standardizing text entries, which improved clarity and removed any potential inconsistencies in categorical data.

5. Handling Missing Data

Where necessary, missing or inconsistent data were addressed. I either filled in with appropriate values or removed incomplete rows to ensure a clean and reliable dataset for analysis.

Final Cleaned Dataset

This clean and structured dataset was the foundation for my exploratory analysis. The steps I took ensured that the dataset was ready for meaningful insights into customer behavior and bike purchasing patterns.

Airbnb Listing and Pricing Analysis for Rental Placement

Overview:

This project uses Tableau to analyze Airbnb data to identify the best locations and property types for maximizing rental revenue. By examining factors such as location, property size, and pricing trends, it offers insights to help hosts make data-driven decisions when starting or managing an Airbnb rental.

Click here to explore the interactive dashboard

Data Summary:

  • Listings Data: Detailed information about Airbnb properties, including descriptions, review scores, neighborhood data, and prices.
  • Calendar Data: Daily availability and pricing for each listing, offering insights into pricing fluctuations and availability patterns over time.

Use Case

A prospective Airbnb host wants to determine the best neighborhood and property size to maximize rental income. They need insights on which areas of Seattle have higher demand, what price points are competitive, and how factors like the number of bedrooms influence booking potential.

Data Cleaning and Preparation

  • Listings Data: Filtered for essential attributes such as listing ID, price, number of bedrooms, and zip code. Null values in critical columns were handled to ensure accurate analysis.
  • Calendar Data: Daily price and availability fields were transformed to focus on active listings and to exclude missing or unavailable dates.

Key Insights and Visualizations

1. Average Airbnb Price by Neighborhood (Zipcode)

This visualization highlights the price variation across different Seattle zip codes. Zip codes like 98121 and 98101 feature higher average prices, making them ideal for luxury or premium rentals.

2. Map of Average Prices by Zipcode

A map showing the average nightly rate for Airbnb listings by zipcode. This gives a visual representation of how location impacts pricing, with some neighborhoods charging over 150% more than others.

3. Revenue Trends Throughout 2016

Revenue trends show peak periods during the summer (July–August), suggesting that the user should consider seasonal fluctuations when setting rental prices.

4. Average Price Per Night by Bedrooms

Properties with more bedrooms command higher prices. For example, 5-bedroom listings average $584.8 per night, compared to $175.4 for 1-bedroom properties.

5. Listings by Bedroom Count

Most listings are 1 to 2 bedroom properties. However, fewer 4 to 5 bedroom properties exist, offering a competitive advantage for users targeting larger group rentals.

Answering Key Questions

1. Which locations offer the highest potential profit for the user?

Zip codes 98121 and 98101 are the most profitable locations due to higher average nightly rates. These areas attract both tourists and business travelers, making them ideal for premium rentals.

2. What type of property should the user invest in to maximize rental income?

Larger properties with more bedrooms tend to command higher nightly rates. Investing in 4 to 5 bedroom properties could generate higher returns, especially in high-demand neighborhoods.

3. When should the user make their rental available to generate the most revenue?

Peak rental periods occur during the summer (July–August), when demand for short-term rentals is highest. It is crucial to make the property available during these times to maximize revenue.

4. What are the key factors that influence Airbnb pricing?

Location and the number of bedrooms are the primary factors affecting Airbnb pricing. Properties in premium zip codes and those with more bedrooms tend to fetch higher prices.

Challenges Faced & Solutions

Managing large datasets required careful filtering to maintain performance in Tableau. Handling missing values in the pricing fields was crucial for ensuring accurate visualizations. Visualization choices such as bar charts, line graphs, and maps effectively communicated insights based on geographical pricing trends and property characteristics.
